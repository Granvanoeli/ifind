California

Gold Rush!!

In 1848 James W. Marshall noticed some shiny metal in the tailrace of a lumber mill at Sutter's Mill (Coloma) and, what do you know? The old dog litterally struck gold! After Marshall's find the news started to spread, and before one was able to saddle a horse and say "giddyup!" thousands came flocking to California.

Yukon - Klondike

The last Great gold Rush!

On August 16 1896 George Carmack and Skookum Jim found a considerable amount of gold at Bonanza creek. Since then and between 1896 and 1899 a stampede of an estimated 100,000 people migrated to the Klondike region of the Yukon in North-West Canada. Wanna have a go? But be careful it's pretty chilly up there!

Scotland - Kildonan

Betcha didn't know Scotland had its own gold rush!

The history of Kildonan's gold started in 1818, when a solitary nugget of gold weighing about ten pennyweights was found in the River Helmsdale. It is claimed a ring was made out of this and is in the possession of the Sutherland family.
